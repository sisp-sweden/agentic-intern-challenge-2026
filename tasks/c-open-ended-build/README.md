# Task C — Build: mentor matching

Fieldnote wants to automate assigning mentors to startups each month. Here's the brief, as it was actually handed to us:

> Startups rank their top 3 preferred mentors. Each mentor can only take on so many startups per month. Build something that produces an assignment.

That's it — that's the whole brief. Treat the gaps as real, because they are; this came from a stakeholder who hadn't fully thought it through yet, which happens often.

## What's here

`starter/mentors.json` and `starter/startups.json` — sample data: mentors with a monthly capacity, startups with a ranked list of their top 3 preferred mentors.

## Your task

Build something that takes this data and produces a mentor assignment for each startup. Language, framework, and format are up to you — a script, a function, an API endpoint, whatever you'd naturally reach for.

You'll notice the brief doesn't say:
- What happens when two startups want the same mentor and only one seat is left
- What a startup gets if all 3 of their preferred mentors are full
- Whether the goal is the most total matches, or the best average preference rank satisfied, or something else
- What the output should look like

That's intentional. Handle it however you think is right — the point isn't which specific approach you pick, it's whether you noticed the gaps and made a real decision about them instead of shipping whatever came out first.
